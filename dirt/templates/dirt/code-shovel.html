Welcome to hammerdirt labs - Python-JS
Visualisation of common statistical indicators

In this section the idea is integrate different methods to convey the meaning of statistical results. The posts will follow release date (kind of like a blog). In the first few posts it will be about integrating the Observable note book (or sections of), how to connect and get data from the API and some basic SVG manipulation.

Inform itelligently
While classic scatter plots are quite clear, not everybody understands a histogram or a distribution plot. It is not our intention to teach the world 'statistics'. The idea here is to convey 'meaning' in the statistical context using JavaScript and Python.
Integrate early
All of what is done here will be used on the app. For those reasons there will be sections devoted to MySQL or Django model-serializers. At the same time the template for generating posts is being developed. So this all going to be used to make this platform a better place. Eventually there will be a user input code or comment section.

Project : Finish the stoplights poster
Comparing percentile rankings

A common evaluation technique is to compare one member of a group to other members of that group. In school this was done on bell curve. The idea is to bring that simplicity to the results of 130 locations across the country with the results from the litter-surveys.

There is math involved
A 'bell curve' is representation of a distribution of values. In the case of the poster the values are pcs/m of trash on the water line. In the probability section that whole model will be treated (but not now)
Inspiration and motivations: 26-09-2018
Trash is a problem everywhere, it ends up in the ocean and fish and people end up eating it. How do you convey the abundance of trash in the natural environment ?
Originally done in Pandas with output to Matplotlob.
