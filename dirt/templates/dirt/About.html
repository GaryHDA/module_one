About hammerdirt:
A service and a concept

Crowd sourced data is important. In general terms it is the state of the environment as documented by those who experience it.

Hammerdirt seeks to present that data objectively and effciently. There is a great potential for citizen science projects and/or crowd sourced data to shape policy and resource allocation in favor of the environment and away from special interests.

That will not happen if the data is locked away in a spreadsheet or in a scientific journal of limited distribution.

A common starting point

Automated systems improve transparency and access. By providing real time analysis and descriptive statistics we hope that discussions can begin and evolve in terms of measured results.

Hammerdirt provides that service by remaining objective and rigorous in data collection methods, presentation and analysis.

How did hammerdirt start ?

After finishing the MBA in 2014, I did an informal survey of local NGO's and other groups active in the environment and specifically trash-littering. I had one question in particular:

How do you measure effectiveness?

I was expecting a metric of some sort that related the project to an actual reduction in litter. There is no such metric and this seems a contradiction if we are serious about reducing our impact. So hammerdirt started on the lake-shore of Lac LÃ©man with a notepad, pen and a pile of collected trash.

By measuring results

Rather than complain about the state of affairs, I chose to develop a platform that can be used to display results and develop analytical tools based on the data as reported by the participants.

Combined, "hammerdirt labs" and the API are an environment to explore analysis methods and visualisations using the full range of tools available.
