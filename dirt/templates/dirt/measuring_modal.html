Measuring beach litter
For a list of further reading on the subject see the bibliography section of the MCBP results.

Beach litter density is the measure of trash on a fixed length of beach or shoreline.

All visible trash is collected, the individual objects are identified and sorted using an international protocol. This process is repeated multiple times per year at the same location.

Although the protocols were written for the marine environment the basic activitiy remains the same. At hammerdirt we have modified the protocol in one important way:

  • We do not require sections of 100m of shoreline

All hammerdirt members have a username and password. Once the objects have been sorted and counted, members can enter the information directly through their smartphones. The results of repeating this multiple times at a variety of locations is what is stored in our database.

It is always best to get the information first hand Guidance on Monitoring of Marine Litter in European Seas
